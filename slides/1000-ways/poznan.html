#magento2performance

Finding the right way

among 1000's wrong

Every efficient solution starts from architecture

Even if your title does not contain a word architect, you are one of them

Rule #1

Minimize amount of I/O operations to the bare minimum

Explain amount of calls to a third-party systems

Evil caching

Rule #2

Make your I/O operations as lightweight as possible

Rule #3

Spend time on the requirement analysis

Rule #4

Analyse the possible data impact

Requirement #1

Your customer asked you to create url slugs for layered navigation. How would you implement it?

Requirement #2

Your customer has 200+ domain names

Each domain has own:

  • Logo
  • Categories
  • Products
  • Homepage

Simplify

Break down your functionality into smallest possible units

  1. Making your functionality parts simple makes it easier to optimize later on
  2. Overcomplicated code is much more harder to optimize
  3. Small units are easier to test

Visualise

Imagine size of affected entities and predict possible index sizes

  • Learn data of your customer before implementation
  • Ask them before it is not too late

Introduce

Create new entities when needed

If store/website does not fit your functionality, create a new entity and use it

Change

Modify default behavior when it does not work for you

If core functionality prohibits you from implementing it in the right way, change it!

Share

Talk with others and think together

  • Talk with other members in your team
  • If they don't know the right solution, they can inspire your for a right one

Open Discussion & Some profiling

Share your specific problem with the room and lets discuss possible ways to solve this
